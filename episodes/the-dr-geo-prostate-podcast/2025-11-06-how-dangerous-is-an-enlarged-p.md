## Summary

Dr. Geo explores why prostate size alone doesn't determine urinary health or danger. The episode clarifies that an enlarged prostate (orange-sized) is distinct from prostate cancer, obstruction can exist without enlargement, and slow urinary flow at dawn may not indicate serious problems. Bladder and nerve function play critical roles alongside prostate size in determining urinary frequency and flow. The discussion covers medication pitfalls, specifically when Finasteride and Dutasteride are prescribed unnecessarily and their hormonal side effects (including hair loss). Testing is also addressed: the ExoDx urine test offers a smarter alternative to PSA screening to avoid unnecessary biopsies, and nerve compression or PAE (prostatic artery embolization) treatment are discussed as examples of when enlargement becomes clinically significant.

Before accepting a diagnosis that requires medication or surgery, ask your urologist whether your prostate size actually correlates with your symptoms, whether bladder or nerve dysfunction might be the real driver, and whether your specific case warrants drug therapy given side effect risks. Request ExoDx testing instead of or before a biopsy if PSA elevation is your main concern. Understand that asymptomatic or mildly symptomatic prostate enlargement rarely requires treatment—the absence of cancer risk and the distinction between size and function are the key takeaways that should frame your treatment conversation with your doctor.

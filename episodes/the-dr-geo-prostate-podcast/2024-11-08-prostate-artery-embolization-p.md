## Summary

**What was discussed:** Dr. Rajpurohit, a clinical professor at NYU Langone, explains Prostate Artery Embolization (PAE) as a minimally invasive alternative to surgery for treating Benign Prostatic Hyperplasia (BPH). The episode covers the science of how PAE works—blocking blood flow to shrink the enlarged prostate—along with documented patient outcomes showing prostate shrinkage and improved urinary symptoms. Key technical details include the choice between radial and femoral artery access routes, post-procedure care protocols, and success rates. The discussion also addresses sexual function outcomes, including effects on ejaculation and erectile function, and compares PAE's effectiveness against other common BPH treatments, with particular emphasis on its efficacy for men with large prostates.

**What's actionable for you:** Determine if you are a candidate for PAE by discussing with an interventional radiologist the factors that make patients suitable for the procedure, and ask specific questions about ejaculatory and erectile preservation, recovery timeline, and long-term outcomes. Seek out PAE specialists—interventional radiologists, not urologists, perform this procedure—through referrals or by contacting academic medical centers like NYU Langone. Use the episode's framework to compare PAE against traditional surgery or medication for your specific prostate size and symptom severity, and consider whether lifestyle modifications alone or combined with treatment options like the Molto prostate massage device (mentioned as FSA/HSA-eligible) align with your preferences before committing to any intervention.

## Summary

Sam Harris discusses American politics with Rahm Emanuel, a veteran Democratic operative who served as Obama's Chief of Staff and Chicago's Mayor, covering the Democratic Party's current state, the 2028 presidential race, and Emanuel's potential candidacy. The conversation spans identity politics and what Emanuel characterizes as the left's cultural "cul-de-sac," antisemitism across the political spectrum, the Gaza war and Netanyahu's failures, U.S.-China policy, wealth inequality, and a bright spot in educational reform called the Mississippi reading miracle.

For listeners considering Democratic leadership options and party direction, Emanuel offers firsthand perspective on what the party needs strategically. For those concerned with antisemitism, Middle East foreign policy, or evaluating whether progressive cultural approaches are working, the episode provides concrete critiques and policy discussion from someone with executive, legislative, and diplomatic experience.

## Transcript

Sam Harris speaks with Rahm Emanuel about American politics, the state of the Democratic Party, and the 2028 presidential race. They discuss Emanuel’s possible presidential run, identity politics and the left’s cultural cul-de-sac, the Mississippi reading miracle, antisemitism on the left and right, the Gaza war, Netanyahu’s failures, U.S. policy toward China, wealth inequality, and other topics.
Rahm I. Emanuel most recently served as the United States Ambassador to Japan. Previously, he was the 55th Mayor of the City of Chicago, a position he held until May 2019. Prior to becoming Mayor, from November 2008 until October 2010, Emanuel served as President Barack Obama’s Chief of Staff. Before serving as Chief of Staff, Emanuel was elected four times as a Member of the U.S. House of Representatives from Illinois’s 5th Congressional District (2002-2008). From 1993 to 1998, Ambassador Emanuel was a key member of President Bill Clinton’s administration, rising to serve as Senior Advisor to the President for Policy and Politics.

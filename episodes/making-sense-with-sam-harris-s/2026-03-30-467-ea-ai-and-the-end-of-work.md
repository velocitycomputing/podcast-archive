## Summary

Sam Harris and William MacAskill discuss effective altruism's recovery following the FTX scandal, examining how to allocate resources most effectively to address global challenges. Their conversation covers pandemic preparedness and global health initiatives, the philosophical limits of quantifiable ethics, artificial intelligence risks including the potential for intelligence explosion and concentrated AI power, and what a post-scarcity world might entail.

For those interested in applying these ideas, explore the organizations MacAskill cofounded: Giving What We Can (which helps donors direct charitable contributions effectively), 80,000 Hours (providing career guidance focused on high-impact work), and the Centre for Effective Altruism. His books—*Doing Good Better*, *Moral Uncertainty*, and *What We Owe The Future*—provide frameworks for thinking through how to maximize positive impact with your time and money in light of existential risks and long-term human flourishing.

## Transcript

Sam Harris speaks with William MacAskill about effective altruism, AI, and the future of humanity. They discuss the post-FTX recovery of the EA movement, global health and pandemic preparedness, the limits of quantifiable ethics, the intelligence explosion, risks of concentrated AI power, what a post-scarcity world might look like, and other topics.
Will MacAskill is an associate professor of moral philosophy at Oxford University, and author of Doing Good Better, Moral Uncertainty and What We Owe The Future. He cofounded the nonprofits 80,000 Hours, Centre for Effective Altruism, and Giving What We Can, and helped to launch the effective altruism movement, which encourages people to use their time and money to support the projects that are most effectively making the world a better place.

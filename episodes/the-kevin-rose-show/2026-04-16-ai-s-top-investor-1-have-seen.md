## Summary

Kevin Rose interviewed Anish Acharya, an A16Z partner and hands-on programmer-investor, covering how AI is reshaping tech and business fundamentals. Key discussion points included why Rose no longer codes, how a personal injury lawyer won the Claude Code hackathon by building software competitive with venture-backed startups, SaaS market decline, and Acharya's provocative prediction that America will achieve a 4-day work week through AI agents and productivity tools rather than cultural discipline—comparing it to how obesity was addressed through Ozempic rather than willpower alone.

The actionable takeaways are: SaaS businesses should reassess their competitive moats as AI commoditizes development; non-technical domain experts (like the lawyer who won the hackathon) now have viable paths to build scalable software without traditional tech backgrounds; AI agents are moving from theoretical to practical deployment; and if you're evaluating your own work/life balance or building a business, expect the 4-day work week conversation to be driven by productivity gains from AI tools rather than industry negotiation. The broader signal: the 1% building with AI are ahead of the 99% still trying to understand what's happening.

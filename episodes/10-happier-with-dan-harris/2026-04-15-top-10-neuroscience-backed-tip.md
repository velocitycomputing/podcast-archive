## Summary

Neuroscientists Amishi Jha and Wendy Suzuki discuss ten evidence-based strategies for strengthening the brain on this 10th-anniversary episode of 10% Happier. The conversation covers neuroplasticity (the brain's ability to change), how exercise directly impacts mood, memory, and attention, the minimum effective dose of meditation for attention improvement, sleep's critical role in memory consolidation and brain cleaning, debunking the multitasking myth, reframing optimization as a burden, using anxiety as a functional tool rather than a purely negative state, and how your daily habits physically shape the brain you'll have tomorrow.

To act on this episode: prioritize sleep, exercise, and meditation as foundational practices—not optional luxuries. Start with meditation's minimum effective dose (you don't need hours) and focus on exercise for its direct cognitive benefits. Stop multitasking and recognize anxiety as a signal you can work with. Most concretely, audit your daily habits now, since they're literally constructing your future brain.

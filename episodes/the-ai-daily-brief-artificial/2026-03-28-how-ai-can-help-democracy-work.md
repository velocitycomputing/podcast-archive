## Summary

Stanford professor Andy Hall argues that AI's potential for democracy lies not in dystopian scenarios but in building tools that enhance citizen intelligence, improve representation, and force greater institutional accountability. NLW explores Hall's essay on "building political superintelligence," presenting the case that AI agents specifically designed for governanceвЂ”rather than purely commercial purposesвЂ”could fundamentally reshape power dynamics and the relationship between people and institutions.

Read Hall's full essay at freesystems.substack.com to understand the mechanics of governance-focused AI agents. Consider how AI tools could be applied within your own civic engagement: improving how you evaluate candidates or policies, enhancing transparency in institutions you interact with, or supporting more faithful representation of your values in decision-making spaces. If you work in policy, civic tech, or governance, this reframes your approach from viewing AI as a potential threat to democracy to treating it as infrastructure for making democratic processes more informed and accountable.

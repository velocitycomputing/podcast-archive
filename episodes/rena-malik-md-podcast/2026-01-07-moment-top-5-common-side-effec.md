## Summary

Dr. Rena Malik and Dr. Aaron Fischman discuss prostate artery embolization (PAE) as a minimally invasive alternative to traditional surgery for benign prostatic hyperplasia (BPH). The episode covers the top five common side effects following the procedure, short-term and long-term outcomes, recovery timelines, activity restrictions, readmission rates for symptom management, long-term success rates, recurrence patterns, expected post-procedural effects, radiation exposure risks, and criteria for identifying ideal PAE candidates. The conversation emphasizes how procedural technique advancements and proper patient selection contribute to better outcomes.

For someone considering PAE, the key actionable takeaways are: understand your expected downtime and activity limitations post-procedure, know the common side effects you may experience and how they're managed, verify whether you meet the criteria as an ideal candidate for the procedure, and ensure your interventionalist has expertise in PAE techniques—provider experience and patient preparation are critical factors determining procedural success. If experiencing complications after PAE, readmission for symptom management is an expected possibility that should be discussed before treatment.
